The Regional Stakeholder Network North East Initiative: The NE, COVID-19 & DISABILITY

A call to help advise Government

We are looking for respondents to our survey: RSN Survey to tell government about how COVID19 is effecting charities in the NE region.

Last year the UK Government launched the Regional Stakeholder Network to bring the voice of the disabled into the heart of Government. The North East of England has the highest percentage of disabled people in the country, making the North East Network one of the most important to be heard. The region is vibrant and diverse communities and their needs are unique and complex.

The UK is currently going through an unprecedented health crisis due to COVID-19. This pandemic is causing severe problems in the North East and is even more severe for the disabled community. The effects upon the disabled and those who support them has been devastating. Since the very start of the pandemic the NE Network has been advising Government on what the issues and problems are here.

Due to the response from the disabled community the NE Network together with the Universities of Northumbria and Newcastle has launched a new and innovative Initiative to seek the views of the wider disabled community in the region. Initially focussed on charities, groups, and local government, this Initiative will be moving to seek the views of individual members of the disabled community. This project is unique and marks a major innovation in how the community can help shape the national strategy. This Initiative is a unique chance for the region’s disabled community to influence national policy. We are seeking help from the community to tell us how COVID-19, and public policy is effecting them now. With the help of the community, charities, groups, public bodies, and above all carers, and the disabled themselves, we can tell Government what the unique issues we as a community are facing and to provide advice on ways to overcome them.

This Initiative is the first attempt of its kind to allow people to help shape policy and regulations that effect them.

As Michael Potts, the NE Chair said,

“The disabled community in the North East, the individuals, carers, and the charities, groups, and public bodies, which support them, is incredibly talented and diverse. They are the experts in how their disability is effecting their own lives. They know only too well how COVID-19 is effecting them. Only charities and groups who support the disabled community everyday know how it is impacting their work and staff. With this ground-breaking Initiative we are seeking to tap into this incredible community and have them tell us how the pandemic is effecting them. Together we can work together to impact their lives. The disabled community needs to be heard and they need to have a voice on things which directly effect them”

Prof. Rob Wilson, Northumbria University, said,

This remains a scary time for many people but particularly those most vulnerable members of the community. These initial findings are only the beginning to working in a longer-term collaboration to put in place infrastructure which supports those providing and needing help to have their voices heard.”

With kind regards,

Michael Potts
Chair of Regional Stakeholder Network North East
